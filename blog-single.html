Preparing Your Site for Winter Weather

By Marcus Chen,Safety Director

Winter in the UK brings specific challenges for construction sites, and scaffolding structures are particularly vulnerable to high winds, ice, and heavy snow loading. Proper preparation isn't just best practice — it's a legal requirement under the Work at Height Regulations 2005.

1. Securing Debris Netting and Sheeting

High winds (exceeding 23mph) place immense lateral force on scaffolding, especially when the structure is wrapped in shrink-wrap or debris netting. Ensure all ties are inspected, and consider reducing the amount of sheeting if extreme gales are forecasted. Our inspectors check wind loading calculations during the 7-day statutory checks.

2. Dealing with Ice on Boards

Frost and ice turn wooden scaffold boards into severe slip hazards. Sites should establish a daily routine of clearing ice before work begins. Using industrial salt on boards is generally not recommended as it can degrade the timber and corrode steel fittings. Instead, use sand or specialized non-corrosive grit.

"In adverse weather, if you are unsure about the safety of the structure, stop work immediately. It is always better to call for a re-inspection than to risk a fall."

3. Post-Storm Re-inspections

Standard 7-day inspections are insufficient during storm season. If your site is hit by gale-force winds, torrential rain, or heavy snow, the scaffold must be re-inspected before anyone uses it again. The Scaffix team provides emergency call-out inspections for exactly this reason.
