Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about scaffolding hire and regulations.

Do I need a permit for my scaffolding?

If the scaffolding needs to be placed on a public highway or pavement, you will legally require a street permit from your local council. Scaffix handles this entire application process for you to ensure full compliance before work begins.

How long does it take to erect scaffolding?

It depends entirely on the size and complexity of the project. A standard residential tower can be erected in a few hours, whereas large commercial projects with temporary roofs or suspended access may take several days or even weeks.

How often is the scaffolding inspected?

By law, scaffolding must be inspected prior to handover, every 7 days while erected, and immediately following adverse weather conditions. Our certified inspectors perform and log all these checks meticulously.
